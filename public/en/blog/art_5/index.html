Brow Center (Myeonggung) and Eyebrow Shapes

The brow center, called Myeonggung (Indang), is considered the primary gate of destiny. Ideally, it should span the width of two fingers, displaying clear, smooth skin. A wide, clean Indang denotes emotional maturity, stress resilience, and open-minded optimism, which invites supportive networks. A narrow, hair-cluttered Indang (brows locking the temple) suggests high anxiety and friction with others. Grooming the brow space and practicing mental relaxation can open your fortune. Brows also represent the sibling/helper sector; neat, long brows reflect social warmth, whereas sparse or cut brows warn against emotional isolation.
